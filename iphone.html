iPhone's history of formation and development

iPhone is a smartphone developed by Apple Inc. It was first introduced by Steve Jobs and released in 2007. Along with the basic features of a mobile phone,the iPhone was equipped with a touch-screen interface, camera, music and video player, and web browser. The second generation, iPhone 3G, was released in July 2008, featuring additional capabilities such as a global positioning system and high-speed 3G network. Over the past 15 years, Apple has produced 34 models of iPhone, from the 2G to iPhone 13 Pro Max, making it one of the most widely known and frequently used smartphone brands in the world.

How many iPhone models are there?

The model codes for iPhones vary depending on the country they are sold in by Apple. For example, LL represents the US, ZA represents Singapore, TH represents Thailand, and JA represents Japan. These model codes are typically used for unofficially imported iPhones in Vietnam, as all officially distributed iPhones in Vietnam are labeled VN/A. However, all of these codes represent genuine Apple products distributed by the company. Using an iPhone with the VN/A code has the advantage of a better 12-month warranty policy according to Apple's standards, which can be serviced at any authorized Apple service center in Vietnam. Certain international model codes may be rejected or not covered by the warranty policy and would require sending the device back to an Apple service center located in a foreign country. As you can see, the process can be quite complicated.
